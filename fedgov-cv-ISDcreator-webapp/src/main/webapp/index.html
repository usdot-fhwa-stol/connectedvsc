    Getting Started - Define your Intersection

    1. Begin by navigating to the File drop down menu. Select New Parent Map or New Child Map.
      • Parent maps only allow reference points that define an intersection location whereas child maps define the features of a given parent map.
    2. If you choose parent map, zoom into the intersection you wish you begin building.
    3. From the top navbar, go to Show Builder to bring up the menu to begin building your intersection.
      • In the Intersection tab, you should see the Reference Point Marker and the Verified Point Marker.
    4. Click and drag the Reference Point Marker to the center of the intersection you wish to begin building.
      • Note that the Markers Control becomes enabled once the marker is placed.
      • You may drag around the marker after being placed to tweak its location
      • Click on the marker to open up the Configuration dialog. From here you can view and modify its information.
      • You may toggle the control back off once you are finished tweaking the location of the markers.
    5. Click and drag the Verified Point Marker to a known, surveyed location on the map.
      • Click on the marker to open up the Configuration dialog. You can view the location of the marker on the map, as well as view and modify the verified location of the marker.
      • Check the verified marker information and edit as necessary. Click Done when finished.
    6. Close the Builder once you are finished placing the two markers.
    7. Continue the process by viewing the Save/Load instruction tab.

    Defining Approaches

    1. Click the Draw Approaches () Control from the bottom Control Panel. The control should turn green when activated.
    2. Drawing an Approach
      1. Click and drag the mouse to create a rectangular box, symbolizing a stop bar, to define an approach. Make the box the approximate size of one of the stop bars in the intersection.
      2. Do not worry about the exact size/shape/position of the box as it can be modified.
    3. Modifying an Approach
      1. Click the Edit Approaches () Control on the bottom Control Panel.
      2. Click the approach box you wish to modify.
      3. Click and drag from the center dot on the approach to move the box around.
      4. Click and drag from the outside dot to rotate and resize the box.
      5. Modify the box as to best fit the stop bar of the intersection.
    4. Configuring an Approach
      1. Verify that all of the Controls on the bottom panel are unselected.
      2. Click on the approach you wish to configure.
      3. In the Approach Configuration dialog, configure as desired.
      4. Click Done when finished.
    5. Repeat the above steps for each approach.
    6. Removing an Approach
      1. Select the Delete Control on the bottom panel.
      2. Click the feature (approach) you wish to delete.
      3. Unselect the Delete Control.

    Defining Lanes

    1. Click the Draw Lanes() Control from the bottom Control Panel.
    2. Drawing a Lane
      1. Place the mouse button inside of the approach box you wish to begin defining lanes.
      2. Click the mouse once to create the first node of the lane.
      3. Click the mouse again to define all subsequent nodes on the lane.
      4. Double-click the mouse on the final node you wish to define on the lane.
    3. Modifying a Lane
      1. Click the Edit Lanes() Control on the bottom Control Panel.
      2. Click the lane you wish to modify.
      3. All of the nodes on the lane will appear. The solid colored nodes are ones already defined. The transparent nodes are ones you can add to the lane by simply dragging them.
      4. Click and drag the nodes around until you have them in the desired position.
    4. Configuring a Lane
      1. Verify that all of the Controls on the bottom panel are unselected.
      2. Click on the lane node you wish to configure in order to bring up the Lane Configuration dialog.
        Note: Only the first node of the lane will hold the Lane Number and Attribute information.
      3. Define a Lane Number (node 1). Modify any other desired settings.
        Note: Lane Attributes will be explained in the next section.
      4. Click Done when finished.
    5. Repeat the above steps for each lane in each approach.
    6. Removing a Lane
      1. Select the Delete Control on the bottom panel.
      2. Click the feature (lane) you wish to delete.
      3. If the lane is a source lane for computed lane's a Dialog will appear warning that deleting the lane will delete all lanes computed from it. Click OK to delete the lane and all of it's computed lanes.
      4. Unselect the Delete Control.

    Note: When defining elevations, keep in mind that the standard defines the delta between elevations to be a range of +/- 5.11 meters. This means that when creating consecutive lane nodes, they should be close enough together to not have an elevation delta outside of the standard's range. The same range applies to lane width delta values.

    Lane Attributes

    • Adding Lane Attributes
      1. Click on the first node of the lane you wish you add attributes to; this will again display the Lane Configuration dialog.
      2. Bring up the Builder and select the Lane tab.
      3. Click and drag the lane attributes from the Builder to the Lane Attributes section of the Lane Configuration.
      4. Let go of the mouse button to "drop" and assign the attribute to the lane.
      5. Click Done when finished adding attributes.
    • Removing Lane Attributes
      1. Click on the first node of the lane you wish you remove attributes from.
      2. From the Lane Attributes section of the Lane Configuration dialog, click and begin to drag the attribute you wish to remove.
      3. Once the dragging begins, a red trashcan will appear at the bottom of the Lane Attributes section.
      4. Hover the attribute over the trashcan and let go of the mouse button to "drop" and remove the attribute from the lane.
      5. Click Done when finished removing attributes.

    Finish and Encode

    Encode your intersection message once you are finished building your intersection.

    • From the top navbar, go to Tools Encoder.
    • Select desired Message Type
      • ISD SDC/SDW Intersection Situation Data
      • Map SAE J2735 MapData message
      • Frame+Map SAE J2735 MessageFrame message with MapData contents
      • SPaT SAE J2735 SPAT message
      • Frame+SPaT SAE J2735 MessageFrame message with SPAT contents
      • SpatRecord ISD message SPaT part
    • Select desired nodes offsets encoding (in the descending order of the message size)
      • Explicit (64 bit) - SAE J2735 Node-LLmD-64b encoding using explicit lat/lon values
      • Standard (32 bit) - SAE J2735 Node-XY-32b offset values
      • Compact determines and uses the same minimal SAE J2735 Node-XY-??b offset value encoding for all nodes
      • Tight - uses the absolute minimal SAE J2735 Node-XY-??b offset value encoding for each node
    • Encode the Message
      1. Press the Encode button to encode your message.
      2. The UPER Hex text area box will display the UPER Hex encoded message if successful, or an error message if unsuccessful.

      Note that content of all text areas can be selected and copied via context (right-click) menu option or using Ctrl-A (Select All) and Ctrl-C (copy) shortcuts.

    Saving/Loading an Intersection

    Use the save feature to save a parent or child map.

    • Saving an Intersection
      1. From the top navbar, go to File Save.
      2. Use your browser dialog to save the generated file to your local machine.
      3. You must save your parent map in order to build a child map.
    • Loading an Intersection
      1. When building a child map, you must always load a parent map. You can also edit child maps using the load function.
      2. Begin by navigating to the File drop down menu. Select New Child Map.
      3. Another dialog window will appear on the screen which allows you to navigate your file system to find the appropriate parent map.
      4. Select and open the parent map.
      5. Use the approaches, lanes, and attributes instructions menus to build the child maps.
    • You can update a child map's reference points by navigating to File Update Child Markers. Use the file navigation to select an updated or new parent map to associate with the child.

    Stencil

    Load a KML file or multiple RSM file(s) to show intersections features not present in a map image. Only one stencil type can be loaded at a time.

    • Loading a Stencil
      1. From the top navbar, go to File Open KML Stencil or go to File Open RSM Stencil .
      2. Use your browser dialog to open the stencil KML file or RSM file(s) from your local machine.
      3. The stencil should appear as a layer on the map.
    • Deleting a Stencil
      1. From the top navbar, go to File Delete Stencil.

    Defining Computed Lanes

    Create a new lane from an existing lane. The computed lane will contain the same configuration as the source lane.

    1. Creating a Computed Lane
      1. Select the source lane that is to be copied.
      2. Click the Create Computed Lane button at the bottom of the source lane's Lane Configuration Lane Info tab
      3. Click the location map where the computed lane is to be created.
      4. The Computed Lane Configuration dialog will open.
      5. On the Computed tab modify the rotation and scalings to be applied as transformations to the source lane's nodes.
        Note: You can modify the offsets as well, but this will create the computed lane in a location different from what was selected on the map.
      6. Modify the other lane configurations as desired.
        Note: The Lane Number will automatically select the next availble value. All other configuration values will be copied from the source lane. All of these values able to be modified.
      7. Click Done when finished.
    2. Modifying a Computed Lane's Form
      1. A computed lane's nodes and their relative positioning will reflect the source lane. To modify the computed lane, click the Edit Lanes() Control on the bottom Control Panel, select the source lane, and modify as desired.
      2. Transformations from the source lane's shape can be made by the computed lane's configuraiton: Click the first node of the computed lane to be modified and update the rotaiton and scalings on the Computed tab of the Computed Lane Configuration
      3. A computed lane's placement on the map can be modified in two ways:
        1. Click the Edit Lanes() Control on the bottom Control Panel and select the computed lane. Click the location on the map where the computed lane is to be moved.
        2. Click the first node of the computed lane to be modified and update the offsets on the Computed tab of the Computed Lane Configuration
    3. Modifying a Computed Lane's Configuration
      1. Verify that all of the Controls on the bottom panel are unselected.
      2. Click on the computed lane node you wish to configure in order to bring up the Computed Lane Configuration dialog.
        Note: Only the first node of the lane will hold the Lane Number and Attribute information.
      3. Modify any desired settings.
        Note: Latitude and Longitude are not able to be modified since they are dependent on the computed transformations applied to the source lane's corresponding node.
      4. Click Done when finished.
    4. Removing a Computed Lane
      1. Select the Delete Control on the bottom panel.
      2. Click the computed lane you wish to delete.
      3. Unselect the Delete Control.
